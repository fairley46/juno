# Exercise: Your Next 30 Days

**Module:** 15 — Troubleshooting and Next Steps

## Learner Objective

Commit to three specific AI use cases from your own work and identify the escalation path if something goes wrong.

## What You'll Do

Plan your first 30 days using AI tools in a structured, intentional way — not open-ended, just three concrete experiments.

## The Task

Identify three real use cases from your work that you'll try with AI in the next 30 days. They can be small. They should be real.

For each one, answer:

**1. What is the task?** (One sentence — specific enough that someone else could do it.)

**2. What's the review step?** (What will you check or do before the output is used or shared?)

**3. How will you know it worked?** (What does success look like?)

**4. Rank your confidence:** High (I feel ready to try this), Medium (I'll try it with a colleague watching), Low (I'd want more guidance before attempting).

Then answer this final question:

**If something goes wrong — the AI produces something harmful, confusing, or that you're not sure what to do with — who's your first call?**

## Completion Criteria

- [ ] Three real use cases are named (not hypothetical or generic)
- [ ] Each has a specific review step (not just "I'd check it")
- [ ] Each has a success definition
- [ ] The learner has named a specific person or team to contact if something goes wrong

## Common Confusion

**"I don't know what I'd try yet."** That's fine — go back to the three workflows from Module 14 and pick the one that sounds most relevant. Start there.

**"All three are 'high confidence.'"** That may be true — but if it is, ask yourself: are you picking tasks that are safe and familiar because you're avoiding the ones where AI would actually save you time? The medium-confidence ones are often the most valuable.

## Facilitator Note

This exercise doubles as a coaching conversation. If the learner can't name three use cases, they haven't connected the training to their actual job — that's useful information. Ask: "What took you the most time last week?" That's usually where the first experiment belongs.

The "who's your first call" question checks whether the escalation contacts from Module 7 stuck. If the learner says "I don't know," remind them that `org/escalation.md` has those contacts and they should bookmark it.
