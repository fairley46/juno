# Exercise: Choose the Right Mode

**Module:** 05 — OpenCode Modes: Plan, Edit, and Agent

## Learner Objective

Select the appropriate OpenCode mode for a given task and describe what human review looks like before allowing the AI to act.

## What You'll Do

Read a task scenario, decide which mode you'd use, and describe your review step before the AI acts.

## The Task

**The scenario:**

You want OpenCode to review a 40-page internal policy document and flag any sections that may conflict with a new regulation your org recently adopted. You'd like it to produce a list of flagged sections with a one-sentence explanation for each.

Answer these questions in this chat:

**Question 1:** Which mode would you use — Plan, Edit, or Agent? Why?

**Question 2:** What would you check or confirm before the AI starts working on this task?

**Question 3:** What does your review look like after the AI produces the flagged list? What's one specific thing you'd verify?

**Bonus question (optional):** Would your answer to Question 1 change if you also wanted the AI to automatically email the flagged list to your legal team when it was done? Why?

## Completion Criteria

- [ ] The learner has selected a mode and explained the reasoning
- [ ] The learner has named a specific pre-task check (not just "I'd make sure it's set up right")
- [ ] The learner has described a specific post-task verification step
- [ ] If they answered the bonus, they've correctly identified why adding the email action changes the risk profile

## Common Confusion

**"I'd just use Agent mode — it can do all of that."** That's technically possible. The question is what the review step looks like and whether you've seen the AI's plan before it acts on 40 pages.

**"Plan mode is just for coding."** Plan mode is relevant any time you want to see what the AI intends before it takes an action — it's not limited to code tasks.

## Facilitator Note

The bonus question tests whether the learner understands that adding an outbound action (email) changes the appropriate oversight level significantly. If they say the mode wouldn't change, ask: "What happens if the AI emails the wrong list or a draft you didn't intend to send?"
